What you will learn

Leadership

Take initiative, make decisions under uncertainty, and guide a team toward a shared goal — not because you're told to, but because you can.

Ownership

Your project, your responsibility. NED teaches students to own their work end-to-end — the wins and the failures.

Problem solving

Break complex problems into parts, test ideas quickly, and iterate. The same method that builds bridges also builds apps.

Communication

Explain your thinking clearly — to a teammate, a teacher, or a room full of people. Ideas only matter if you can share them.

Teamwork

Build with other people. Disagree productively. Divide work fairly. The best projects are never solo — and neither are the best careers.

Doing hard things

Experiments fail. Code breaks. That's the point. NED students learn to push through frustration and come out sharper on the other side.

Teacher training

Great STEM education starts with great teachers

Africa doesn't just have a student access problem — it has a teacher readiness problem. Most science teachers were never trained to teach STEM the way the world now demands it: hands-on, project-based, and connected to real careers. NED bridges that gap.

Curriculum redesign

We work with educators to restructure STEM curricula around practical application, local context, and career relevance — not just exam prep.

Hands-on methodology

Intensive workshops that equip teachers to lead experiments, build circuits, and run coding sessions — even with limited lab resources.

Train-the-trainer

We don't just train once and leave. Our cascading model turns your best teachers into STEM training leads who upskill their peers long-term.

Impact measurement

Pre- and post-training assessments, classroom observation, and reporting dashboards so funders and agencies can see measurable outcomes.

Regional deployment

Programmes designed for state-wide or regional rollout. We've built NED to scale across diverse school systems and resource levels.

Certification

Teachers who complete the programme earn a NED STEM Educator certification — a verifiable credential that signals readiness to teach modern STEM.

Community initiative

Solve real problems. Build what matters.

NED Community is for university students and recent graduates who want to apply technology — hardware or software — to local challenges. This isn't coursework. It's real projects, real communities, real impact.

Hardware

Build devices that solve local problems

Solar-powered irrigation controllers, air quality monitors, low-cost medical devices — prototype solutions for challenges in your community.

Software

Write code that changes how things work

Apps for local market traders, school management tools, health data platforms — build software that makes a tangible difference where you live.

Research

Investigate and document what's needed

Community research, needs assessment, and feasibility studies that ground every project in evidence and local context.

Mentorship

Learn from people who've built before

Pair with engineers, designers, and entrepreneurs who guide your project from idea to working prototype.
